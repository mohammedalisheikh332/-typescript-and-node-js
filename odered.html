Shahid Afridi
Shahid Afridi, also known as "Boom Boom Afridi," is a former Pakistani cricketer renowned for his aggressive batting style and leg-spin bowling. Born on March 1, 1980, in Khyber Agency, Pakistan, Afridi became famous for his fast scoring and holds the record for one of the fastest centuries in ODI cricket, made from just 37 balls. He was a key player for Pakistan in all formats of the game, particularly known for his performances in limited-overs cricket. Afridi also captained the Pakistan national team in various periods and was instrumental in Pakistan's victory in the 2009 ICC World Twenty20.
